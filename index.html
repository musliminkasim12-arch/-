About the B-2 Spirit

The B-2 Spirit is a long-range strategic stealth bomber used by the United States Air Force. Its flying-wing design and radar-absorbing materials make it extremely difficult to detect.

History and Development

The aircraft first flew on July 17, 1989, and officially entered service in 1997. It was developed during the Cold War to penetrate advanced air defense systems.

Who Invented It?

The B-2 was developed by Northrop Corporation (now Northrop Grumman) for the United States Air Force, using advanced stealth and aerospace engineering technology.

What It Can Do

  • Evade radar using stealth technology
  • Fly over 6,000 nautical miles without refueling
  • Carry conventional and nuclear payloads
  • Deliver precision-guided weapons
  • Perform global strike missions

Basic Specifications

  • First Flight: July 17, 1989
  • Introduced: 1997
  • Crew: 2 pilots
  • Wingspan: 172 feet (52.4 meters)
  • Manufacturer: Northrop Grumman
